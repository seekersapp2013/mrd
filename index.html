We Offer Different Services To Improve Your Health

MRI

MRI is an advanced medical imaging procedure that uses a powerful magnetic field, radio waves and a computer to create cross-sectional images.

Computed Tomography

Computed tomography (CT) helps differentiate between healthy and diseased tissue in any part of the body, making it possible to accurately diagnose many diseases at an early stage.

Digital Mammography

Mammography is a type of low-dose X-ray of the breast. It reveals masses and micro-calcifications within and around the breast that may indicate breast problems.

Digital X-Ray

X-ray is the most frequently used form of medical imaging worldwide. It is used to diagnose disease, monitor therapy, and identify potential problems throughout our bodies.

Fluoroscopy

Fluoroscopy uses a continuous low-dose X-ray beam, often combined with a contrast material (dye), to produce images of organs and bones in real time.

Ultrasound

Ultrasound uses sound waves and a computer to create images of internal organs and blood vessels and to monitor health conditions.

Endoscopy

“Endoscopy is a procedure where organs inside your body are examined using an instrument called an endoscope.”

Endoscopy is used in diagnosis, most commonly by performing a biopsy to check for conditions such as anemia, bleeding, inflammation, and cancers of the digestive system. Endoscopy is commonly used for:

  • Diagnostic evaluation of symptoms
  • Cancer prevention
  • Biopsy of an abnormal finding on a chest X-ray.

Radiology

“Have you ever thought about the technology that diagnostic imaging centers use?”

Radiology is the area of medicine that uses X-rays, radioactive tracers, magnetic waves and ultrasonic waves to obtain detailed images of the inside of the body.

We have several imaging tests at Medicaid Radio-diagnostics

  • Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)
  • Computer Tomography (CT)
  • Ultrasound Scan

Echocardiogram

Echocardiogram is key in determining the health of the heart muscle, especially after a heart attack. It can also reveal heart defects in unborn babies.

Echocardiography is a test that uses sound waves to produce live images of your heart. The image is called an echocardiogram. This test allows your doctor to monitor how your heart and its valves are functioning. The images help doctors get information about:

  • Problems with the function of your heart valves.
  • Problems with the pumping function or relaxing function of the heart.
  • Blood clots in the heart chambers
